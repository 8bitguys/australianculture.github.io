The Pre-British Era

Before the British came to Australia, it was the Aboriginees who had settled here. It is believed that they had arrived in Australia 40,000-70,000 years ago. They started to build up their civilization. They developed a kind of 'hunter-gatherer' lifestyle. They used 'Stone Technology' or tools made out of stone to evolve and survive. When the British arrived, it was estimated that there was a population of around 350,000 to a whopping 750,000 people. They had lots of belief systems like worshipping and sacrifices. The population dropped to an whopping 93,200(Approximately) in 1900. However, this has changed as of research in 2011. There was a huge increase and it went back to 670,000(Approx.) It is estimated that it will increase up to 721,000 if the annual growth rate(2.2%)
